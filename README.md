## ✨ What it does

Your Discord profile shows a real, green **Playing Grand Theft Auto VI** activity — with the
game's art, a sub-title, a ticking timer, and a clickable button — exactly like you'd see for
someone actually in the game. Your friends have no way to tell it's fake from your profile.

- 🎮 Real **“Playing” status** (uses Discord's official Rich Presence — the same thing real games use)
- ⚡ **One click** — no login, no account, no setup
- 🎨 **Custom troll text** — edit the two lines shown under the game
- ⏱️ Live elapsed **timer** and a **button** on your profile
- 🪶 Tiny single `.exe`, nothing to install

## ⬇️ Download & run

1. Grab **`PresenceFaker.exe`** from the [latest release](https://github.com/Bettal-Hamza/presencefaker/releases/latest).
2. Windows may say **“unknown publisher”** — that's normal for free indie apps. Click
   **More info → Run anyway**.
3. Make sure the **Discord desktop app is open** and you're logged in.
4. Open PresenceFaker and hit **Start**. Your profile instantly shows *Playing Grand Theft Auto VI*.
5. Hit **Stop** (or just close it) to turn the prank off.

## 💻 Requirements

- Windows 10 / 11
- The **Discord desktop app** (open and logged in)
- Does **not** work in the Discord browser/phone app — it needs the desktop app's local connection.

## 🔒 Is it safe? How does it work?

Yes. PresenceFaker talks to your **already-running Discord** through its official **Rich Presence**
channel — the exact system games use to display what you're playing. It:

- **never** sees or touches your password or login token,
- **doesn't** sign in or modify your account,
- **collects no data**, and
- **isn't** a hack, cheat, or mod.

It simply tells Discord *“I'm playing this game.”* Close the app and the status disappears.

## ❓ FAQ

**Do I need to own GTA VI?** No — that's the whole joke. It shows the status without the game.

**Will my friends know it's fake?** Not from your profile. It's identical to actually playing.

**Does it work on mobile or the browser?** No, Windows desktop only.

**Why does Windows warn me?** The app isn't code-signed yet (signing certificates cost money).
The warning is just Windows being cautious about unknown developers — click *More info → Run anyway*.
